What is Meant by Rounding to the Nearest Ten Millionths?

In maths, rounding means trimming the numbers to the nearest place value. Rounding to the nearest ten-millionths means trimming the decimals to the closest ten millionth place value. The ten million number after the decimal point is defined as 1/10000000 (one-ten-millionth). Also, the seventh position after the decimal point is the ten-millionth place.

Steps to Round Off Decimals to the Nearest 10000000th

For rounding decimals to the nearest ten millionth, we have to follow some rounding rules and steps that are listed below:

  • First, identify the ten-millionth digit.
  • Next, identify the hundred millionth digit.
  • To round off decimal to 10000000th, we have to look at the hundred millionth digit.
  • If the digit >=5 then round up the number to the nearest ten millionths.
  • If the digit <5 then round down the number to the nearest ten millionths.
  • Finally, the obtained result is the rounded value to the nearest 10000000th.
