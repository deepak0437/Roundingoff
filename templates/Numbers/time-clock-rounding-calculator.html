What is Time Clock Rounding?

In a nutshell Time Clock Rounding is nothing but the process of rounding up or rounding down by set increments. Depending on the situation you can choose from any of the time clock roundings available. Common Clock Time Roundings include 5-minute increments, 6-minute increments, 15-minute increments or quarter hour rounding, etc.

Common Clock Time Rounding Methods & Their Rules?

There are several rounding methods available to round the clock time. However, we have outlined some of the common and most popularly used rounding techniques in the below modules. They are as under

15-Minute Rounding: In case of 15-Minute Time Clock Rounding or Quarter Hour Time Clock Rounding we will keep two thresholds for clock-ins and clock-outs whether to round up or round down. For the first 7.5 minutes we will round down and for the second 7.5 minutes we will round up to full quarter of an hour. For example: 15.8 rounded to 15 minutes is 15.15, 6.05 rounded to 15 minutes is 6.00

5-Minute Rounding: Just like 15-minute rounding process, 5-minute rounding method also splits every 5 minutes into 2 parts of 2.5 minutes. For the first 2.5 minutes clock-in time we will round down and for the second 2.5 minutes we will round up to the nearest 5 minutes. For example: 8.03 rounded to 5-minutes is 8.05 and 17.02 rounded to 5-minutes is 17.00

6-Minutes Rounding or Rounding to 1/10th of an Hour: Rounding to 1/10th of an hour uses 3-minute intervals. For the first 3-minutes we will round down the given time and for the second half of 3-minutes is rounded up to nearest 6 minutes. For example 7:28 rounded to nearest 6 minutes is 8:00 and 9:03 rounded to nearest 6 minutes is 9:00
