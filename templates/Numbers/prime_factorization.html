What is meant by Prime Factorization?

In Maths, Prime Factorization is the method to find prime numbers of a given number. When all the Prime Factors of the numbers are multiplied you will get the original number. Prime Numbers are the basic blocks of all numbers. We know Prime number is the number divided by 1 and the number itself. The Method of Prime Factorization is used to break down the whole number into its prime factors. Prime Factorization is also known as Prime Decomposition.

How to find Prime Factorization of a number?

Prime factorization is the decomposition of composite numbers into a product of prime numbers. There are many methods to do the Prime Factorization and we have listed some of them namely Trial Division and Factor Tree Methods. Check out them to have an idea of them in detail so that you can choose the method you want.

Trial Division

One of the methods to obtain the Prime Factors of a number is through trial division. It is one of the basic algorithms and can be highly tedious. Start by testing each integer to see if and how it divides the number and the subsequent quotients evenly. The resulting set of factors will be prime numbers.

Factor Tree

Factor Tree Diagram is an easy way to break down a number into its prime factors.

  • Take a number in the first step.
  • Factor it into any two numbers and you may need it to split it easily.
  • Start constructing the factor tree i.e. draw two branches splitting down the original number.
  • Factor the next line. If your number is prime leave it as it is or else factor the numbers again.
  • Continue the Process until you are left with all the prime numbers.
  • Write down the Prime Factorization by taking all the leaves of the factor tree.
