How to Round to the Nearest Inch?

The following is the simple procedure to round off a number to its nearest inch value easily. Check out the instructions, guidelines, rounding rules and follow them to get the answer effortlessly.

  • Take any number that is having fractional part.
  • Check wheather the fractional part of the nummber is less than 1/2.
  • If it is less than 1/2, then remove fractional part and remaining number becomes the nearest inch value.
  • When the fractional part is equal to or more than 1/2, then add 1 to the integer part and that is the nearest inch value.

Example

Question: What is 2 (3/5)" rounded to the nearest inch?

Solution:

Given number is 2 (3/5)"

Here the fractional part is 3/5

3/5 is more than 1/2. So, add 1 to the integer part

2+1 = 3

Therefore, 2 (3/5)" rounded to the nearest inch is 3.

FAQ's on Round to the Nearest Inch Calculator

1. How do you round to the nearest inch?

To round a number to the nearest inch is check the fractional part. If the fractional part is 1/2 or more than that, then round up. If the fractional part is less than 1/2, then round down.

2. What does rounding to the nearest inch mean?

Rounding to the nearest inch means estimating the given number value and making it simpler to round but keeping its value closer to what it is.

3. What is the difference between round up and round down?

Round up means making the given number equal to the next smallest inch that is larger than the given number. Round down means making that decimal number equal to the next larger integer that is smaller than the given number.
