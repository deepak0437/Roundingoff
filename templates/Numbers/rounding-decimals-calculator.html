What is Meant by Rounding Decimals in Maths?

In Maths, Rounding decimals outline the method of rounding a decimal number to a certain degree of precision. We may round decimals to the nearest whole number or nearest tenths, nearest hundredth, and so on.

Whenever you round off the decimal number, take the next digit in the right place. If the digit value is greater than 5, then round up and if the digit is less than 5, round down.

For Example, round off the decimal number 11.345 to the nearest whole number. Here, consider the tenth place value which is 3. The value is less than 5, so the result of the given decimal 11.345 is 11. If the given decimal is 11.647, then the result of rounding decimals is 12 because the value in tenths place ie., 6 is greater than 5.

How to Round Off Decimals to Nearest Whole Number?

  1. Initially, you need to check the tenths place value in the given decimal.
  2. In case, it is equal to 5 or more than 5 then raise the whole number's one's place by 1 and eliminate the decimal point and the digits after it.
  3. If it is equal to 4 or less than 5, then just eliminate all the digits along with the decimal separator.

Example:

Round off 456.89 to the nearest whole number.

Solution:

The value in tenths place is greater than 5 ie., 8 and in one's place is 6.

Now, add 1 unit to the one's digit ie. 456.89 to 457.89 and remove the decimals points.

Therefore, the round off 456.89 to nearest whole number is 457

How to Rounding Off a Decimal Number to Tenths?

  1. Firstly, look at the given decimal and then verify the hundredths place value.
  2. If the value in hundredths digit is less than 5 then round down the number to tenths place.
  3. If the digit is greater than 5 then round up the number to the nearest tenths.

Steps to Rounding Decimal to the Nearest Hundredths 

The values that should be addressed to two places after decimal separator is said to be as Rounding off to the nearest hundredths. Follow the steps and find the round off decimal to nearest hundredths:

  1. First, we consider the number that we need to round and then look for the digit in the hundredths place to round off to nearest hundredths.
  2. Now, check the digit to the right of the hundredths column ie., thousandths place.
    • (i) If the digit in the thousandths place is 0, 1, 2, 3 or 4, we will round down the hundredths place to the nearest hundredths.
    • (ii) If the digit in the thousandths column is 5, 6, 7, 8 or 9, we will round up the hundredths place to the nearest hundredths.
  3. Eliminate all the digits to the right of the hundredths place for the exact result.

Example:

Rounding Off Decimal Number 4.762

Solution:

Consider the number 4.762 and round off to nearest hundredth

Here, 6 is followed by 2 and 2 is less than 5.

Hence, 4.762 rounded to nearest hundredth is 4.75
