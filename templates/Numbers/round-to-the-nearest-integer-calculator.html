What does it mean by Rounding a Number?

Rounding is the process to keep a number short or simple and making sure the rounded value is as close as possible to the original number.

How to Round to the Nearest Integer?

Follow the simple steps listed below to round to the nearest integer and they are as under

  • The trick is simple firstly look at the digits in the tenths place.
  • If the digit in the tenths place is less than 5 simply round down the number i.e. units digit is left unaltered.
  • if the digit in the tenths place is greater than 5 simply round up and increase the units digit by one.

Example

Question : What is Decimal Number 51.85 rounded to the nearest integer?

Solution:

Firstly, look at the tenths place of the decimal number 51.85

Here it is 8 i.e. greater than 5 so we will round up the units digit by one

Increasing the units digit of the decimal number by 1 we have the value as 52

Therefore, 51.85 rounded to nearest integer is 52.

FAQs on Round to the Nearest Integer

1. How do you round decimal numbers to nearest integer?

You can round decimal numbers to nearest integers either by rounding up or rounding down based on the tenths place. If the tenths place is greater than 5 we will round up the units digit by one and if it is less than 5 we will round down and the units digit remains same.

2. What is 5.32 rounded to nearest integer?

5.32 rounded to nearest integer is 5 as the tenths place digit is 3 and it is closer to 5 than 6.
