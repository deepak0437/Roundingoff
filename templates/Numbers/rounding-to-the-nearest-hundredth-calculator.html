What is meant by Rounding to the Nearest 100th?

In mathematics, Decimal numbers are the numbers that are in reference to the preceding powers of 10. It implies that, if we move from left to right, the place value of the decimal number divides by 10.

As a result, it determines tenths, hundredths, thousandths, and so on. Here in decimals, hundredth means 1/100. Rounding to the nearest hundredth is finding the rounded value nearest to the 100th value by following the rounding decimal rules.

For example, 6.1342 is the given decimal and the rounded value to the nearest hundredth is 6.13. Want to learn the process and do calculations on your own then refer to the below steps with examples.

Detailed Steps to Round off to Nearest Hundredth by Hand

Here are the simple steps that you should focus on rounding off numbers to the nearest hundredth manually:

  • First, identify the number in the hundredth place.
  • Now, observe the thousandths digit which is next to 100th place.
  • If the digit is more than or equal to 5, round up the 100th value.
  • If the digit is less than 5, round down the hundredth value by remaining the same digit in the hundredth place and remove all the digits right to the hundredth place.

Example:

Round 93.1857 to the Nearest Hundredth?

Solution:

Given decimal is 93. 1857

Here, 8 is the hundredth value and 5 is the thousandth value.

The thousandth value is equal to 5 so round up to the 100th value by adding 1 to the hundredth place.

Hence, 93.1857 rounded to the Nearest Hundredth is 93.19.
