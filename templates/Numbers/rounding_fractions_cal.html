Steps to Round Fractions

Follow the below provided steps to rounding off fractions to the nearest whole number or nearest tenths, hundredths and thousandth number.

  • Take any fraction.
  • Convert the given fraction into decimal by dividing numerator by denominator.
  • If the obtained number to the right of the decimal point is 0.5 or more, then add 1 to the left of the decimal point and remove the decimal part.
  • If the obtained number to the right of the decimal point is lesser than 0.5, then leave the number to left of decimal point and remove the decimal part.
  • Write the new number as rounded number.

Example

Question : What is 15/8 is rounded to the nearest whole number?

Solution:

Given fraction is 15/8

15/8 = 1.875

The number right of the decimal point is .8 which is greater than .5. So, add 1 to the left of the decimal point and remove decimal part.

1 + 1 = 2

Therefore, the rounded 15/8 to the nearest whole number is 2.

FAQs on Rounding Fractions Calculator

1. How to round a fraction to the nearest whole number?

To round a fraction to the nearest whole number, find the decimal value of fraction. And follow the rounding steps to get the whole number form.

2. What is meant by rounding fractions?

Rounding fractions is a kind of estimating them in the rounded form rather than the exact form. The rules for rounding numbers are if the digit is less than 5, don't change the rounding figure and replace all the digits right to it as zero. If the digit is gretaer than 5, add 1 to the rounding digit and place all the digits right to it as zeros.

3. How do you round fractions on a calculator?

4. What is fraction 5/8 rounded to the nearest tenths?

The decimal value of 5/8 is 0.625

The primary rule in rounding to the nearest tenths is check the hundredths digit i.e 2

As 2 is less than 5, tenths digit remains same.

So, rounded 5/8 to the nearest tenths is 0.6
