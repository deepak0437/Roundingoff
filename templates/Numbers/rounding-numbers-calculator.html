Rules for Rounding Numbers

Adhering to the rules of the rounding off numbers are given below. Have a look at the list and learn how to round numbers to certain unit places:

Rule 1: Any non-zero digit in a number and any zero that seems between non-zero digits are deemed as significant.
Rule 2: Let's assume we have a whole number, the zero that is positioned to the right of a non-zero digit is important.
Rule 3: If the rounding digit is less than 5, then the digit that follows is left unchanged.
Rule 4: If the rounding digit is greater than or equal to 5, then the digit that follows is increased by 1.
Rule 5: Suppose, rounding number has a higher place value (hundreds, thousands, and so on) then the lower place value digits are ignored.

Rounding Off Numbers to the Nearest Whole Number?

  • In order to round off whole numbers, first, find the place value that you want to round, and just see the digit just right to it.
  • If that digit number is less than 5, don’t change the rounding figure, but replace all the digits right to the rounding digits to “zero”.
  • If it is greater than 5, add 1 to the rounding digit, and replace all the digits right to the rounding digits to “zero”.

Process to Round Numbers to Nearest Tens

  • Recognize the tens digit: the first 9 in 569
  • Consider the next value which is 9 in 569
  • If the digit is more than or equal to 5 then round up.
  • The tens digit increases by one. Since 6+1=7, Each digit after becomes a zero.
  • 569 rounded to the nearest ten is 570.

Example:

Find Round Off 123725 to the nearest tens place.

Solution:

Given number is 123725

The digit in the Ones Place is 5 (greater than equal to 5). Therefore, we add 1 to the previous digit 2. All digits after that number becomes a zero. 

Round off 123725 at Tens Place = 123730

Steps to Round Off Numbers to Nearest Hundreds

Here are the simple steps that are explained with example to rounding numbers to the nearest hundred.

  • To Round the Number 4270 to the Nearest Hundred.
  • First, find the hundred digit (here it is 2)
  • Now, take the tens place value (it is 7)
  • If the digit is greater than or equal to 5 then you should round up.
  • Raise the hundreds digit by one, so 2 becomes 3. Every digit after becomes a zero.
  • Hence, the number 4270 rounded to nearest hundred is 4300.
