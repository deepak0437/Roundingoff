What is Meant by Rounding to the Nearest Ten Thousandths?

The rounded value of the Decimal numbers to the nearest 1/10000 (one-ten-thousandth) is called rounding to the nearest ten thousandth. The fourth position after the decimal point is the place of ten thousandth value. Take a look at the below image and understand its location.

For example, 5134.43235 is a decimal number, find the rounded value to the nearest 10000th. 3 is the ten thousandth digit. After applying the rules of rounding decimals to the nearest ten thousandth for the value 5134.43235, the rounded value is 5134.4324.

How to Round Off to the Nearest 10000th (Step-by-Step)?

Have a glance at the manual process of rounding off decimals to the nearest ten thousandth and get familiar with the calculations. Here, we have listed out the simple steps to round decimals to the nearest 10000th by following the rounding rules. So, refer to them carefully and make your calculations easy.

  • Initially, recognize the ten thousandth place value from the given decimal (which is the 4th digit after the decimal point).
  • Next, identify the 5th digit after the decimal point which is hundred thousandth value to round off the number to the nearest 10000th.
  • If the 5th digit is >=5 then round up the number to the nearest ten thousandth.
  • If the 5th digit is <5 then round down the number to the nearest 10000th.

1. What is the ten thousandths place?

The nearest ten thosandths place is the fourth digit to the right of the decimal point.

2. How do you round to the nearest ten thousandths?

To round the decimal number to its nearest ten thousandths, look at the hundredth thousandths number. If it is more than or equal to 5, add 1 to the 10000th value. In case, it is less than 5, keep the value in ten thousandths place same and eliminate all the numbers next to the 10000th place.

3. What is the ten thousandth in decimals?

In decimals, ten thousandth is one part in ten thousand equal parts ie., 1/10,000 (one-ten-thousandth).
