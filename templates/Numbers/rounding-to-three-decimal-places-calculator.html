What does Rounding Off to Three Decimal Places Mean?

Rounding to Three Decimal Places is the process of rounding to thousandths place i.e. 3nd place to the right of decimal point. For Example 5.5380 rounded to three decimal places is 5.538

Rules for Rounding to Three Decimal Places

For rounding to three decimal places you need to follow certain rules. They are explained in the further modules

  • Firstly visit the decimal point and count 3 places to the right.
  • Look at the next number after 3 decimal places i.e. 4th decimal place or ten thousandths place.
  • If the ten thousandths place digit is greater than or equal to 5 simply round up and add 1 to thousandths place.
  • If the ten thousandths place digit is less than or equal to 4 simply we will round down and keep the thousandths place digit unchanged.

Example of Rounding to the Three Decimal Places

Example 1: What is 12.35824 rounded to three decimal places?

Solution:

Given Number is 12.35824

First step is to identify the decimal point and count 3 decimal places from it.

Look at the 4th decimal place i.e. ten thousandths if it is >=5 simply round up or else round down. Here it is 2

Since 2 < 5 e will round down and keep the thousandths place same.

Therefore, 12.35824 rounded to 3 decimal places is 12.358

Example 2: Round 17.9566 to the Two Decimal Places

Solution:

Given Number is 17.9566

Identify the decimal point and count 3 decimal places from it.

Check the 4th decimal place. If it is >=5 round up or else round down. Here it is 6

Since 6 > 5 we will round up and increase the 3rd decimal place by 1 i.e. 6+1 = 7

Therefore, 17.9566 rounded to three decimal places is 17.957
