Process to Rounding Fractions To the Nearest Half

The following step by step process is useful to round fractions to the nearest half.

  • Let us take any fraction and divide the numerator value by denominator.
  • Now you will get a decimal number.
  • Use rounding rules to round to fraction to the nearest half.

Example

Question: What is 68/5 is rounded to the nearest half?

Solution:

Given fraction is 68/5

68/5 = 13.6

The number right of the decimal point is .6 which is greater than .5. So, add 1 to the left of the decimal point and remove decimal part.

13 + 1 = 14

Therefore, the rounded 68/5 to the nearest half is 14.

Frequently Asked Question's on Rounding Fractions

1. What is round half up?

Rounding up means adding 1 to the rounding digit and placing the numbers right to the rounding digits as zeros. The common method of rounding is to make 0.5 go up.

2. How do you round a fraction to the nearest half?

When rounding to the nearest half, round the fraction to whichever half the fraction is closest number. When a fraction is close to two different halves, then round fraction up.

3. Is 1/2 rounded up or down?

We already know that 1/2 = 0.5. So the fraction 1/2 is rounded up.
