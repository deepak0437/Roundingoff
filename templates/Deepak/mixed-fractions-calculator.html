A mixed number is a whole number, and a proper fraction represented together. It generally represents a number between any two whole numbers. A mixed number is formed by combining three parts: a whole number, a numerator, and a denominator. The numerator and denominator are part of the proper fraction that makes the mixed number.


Steps to follow to converting as mixed fraction

  1. Divide the numerator by the denominator

  2. Write down the quotient as the whole number

  3. Write down the remainder as the numerator and the divisor as the denominator
