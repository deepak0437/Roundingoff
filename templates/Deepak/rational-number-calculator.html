Definition of Rational Number

In maths, the definition of Rational Number is the number that is expressed in the form of p/q where q ≠ 0. 

What is the Standard Form of a Rational Number?

Generally, the standard form of a rational number is a/b. Do remember it should not have common factors other than 1 between the numerator and denominator besides the denominator b should be positive always.

How to Find or Identify Given Number is Rational Number? 

To find if the given number is rational or not manually, you have to verify the below conditions is matching or not:

  • The number is in the standard form or not ie., p/q where q≠0.
  • Rational numbers includes positive, negative numbers, and zero. 
  • Also, it can be expressed as a fraction. 
  • Decimal numbers can also be called rational numbers as it can be converted into fractional form too.
