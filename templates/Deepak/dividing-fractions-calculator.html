In maths, the representation of part of the whole is called Fractions. The definition of a fraction is the ratio of numerator to denominator. The number on the slash ‘/’ symbol is called the numerator and the other is called the denominator.
There are several types of fractions like a proper fraction, improper fraction, mixed fraction, like fractions, unlike fractions, and so on. The division of two fractions can result in fraction form or decimal form or whole number.

Dividing Fractions Formula.

The direct formula we can use to divide the fractions is here. Let’s see if a/b and c/d are the two fractions, then the division of the two fractions is expressed by:

a/b ÷ c/d = ad/bc

a = dividend numerator

b = dividend denominator

c = divisor numerator

d = divisor denominator

How Do You Divide Fractions Manually?

The steps that we need to follow to divide fractions manually are given below:

  • Firstly, to divide the fractions we need to gather all inputs in numerator and denominator form.

  • After that, change the second fraction to reciprocal.

  • Now, take the two numerators and two denominators together and multiply them.

  • Simply the expression and get the end result in the simplified form of the fraction.

  • If you need the result in decimal form, then divide the numerator by the denominator from the fraction output
