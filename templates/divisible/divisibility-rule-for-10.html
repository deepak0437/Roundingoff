Divisibility Rule for 10

The test of divisibility for 10 states that any number which ends with 0 is divisible by 10.

Example: 10, 100, 20, 250, and so on are divisible by 10

Manual Steps to Check Divisibility by 10

Following are the simple steps to tell whether any whole number is divisible by 10 or not.

  • The simple technique is to check the last digit of the number.
  • If it is 0, then obviously the given number is divisible by 10.
  • Otherwise, the number is not divisible by 10.

FAQs on Divisibility of 10

1. What is the divisibility rule of 10?

The divisibility rule of 10 stays that any number whose last digit is 0 is divisible by 10.

2. What number is not divisible by 10?

Numbers that don't end with zero are not divisible by 10. Examples are 21, 57, 128, 503, and so on.

3. Why is the divisibility test for 10 valid?

The divisibility test for 10 is valid because the multiples of 10 have zero as the ending digit.

4. Is 100 divisible by 10?

Yes, 100 is divisible by 10 as it has 0 at the unit's place.
