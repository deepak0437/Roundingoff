What is the Divisibility Rule of 6?

Divisibility Rule of 6 states that a given number is divisible by 6 when it is divisible by 2 and 3. To make it divisible, we have to use the divisibility test of 2 and the divisibility test of 3. Also, the number is divisible by 6 if it is the whole number.

There are two conditions that a whole number should meet to be divisible by 6. They are given below:

  • The required whole number needs to be divisible by 2 and it can happen when the unit place digit is even ie., it should end with 0, 2, 4, 6, and 8.
  • One more condition is it should be divisible by 3. For this, a number is divisible by 3 if the sum of the digits of the given number is divisible by 3.

While checking whether the number is divisible by 6, it should satisfy both conditions. If any of the conditions is failed then the given number is not divisible by 6. Also, all the even numbers included in the 3 tables are divisible by 6.

Solved Example on How To Calculate Divisibility by 6?

Example:

Let's check that 5124 is divisible by 6 by applying the divisibility test of 6.

Solution:

Given number is 5124

To check if 5124 is divisible by 6 or not, apply the divisibility test by 6:

  1. 5124 is an even number. As it ends with an even number then the number is divisible by 2. Hence, condition 1 is meeting.
  1. Now, add all the digits of the number and check whether the result is divisible by 3 or not. Therefore, (5+1+2+4) = 12. The sum of 12 is divisible by 3. Hence, condition 2 is satisfied.

Since 5124 is divisible by both 2 and 3, then the number 5124 is divisible by 6.
