Divisibility Rule for 14

The divisibility test of 14 checks of the number is divisible by 14 or not. If any number is completely divisible by 14 then the quotient will be a whole number and the remainder is zero. In simple words, the divisibility rule for 14 says that if a number is divisible by 14 then it should be divisible by both 7 and 2. Check the complete information about the test of divisibility for 14, the steps on how to prove it and solved questions.

What is the divisibility rule of 14?

Any even number which is divisible by 7 is divisible by 14. The divisibility of 7 states that subtract the double of the last digit from the remaining digits of the number. If the difference is a multiple of 7, then the number is divisible by 7.

Example: Take number 224.

Subtraction = 22 - (4 x 2) = 22 - 8 = 14

14 is divisible by 14

So, 224 is divisible by 7 and it is divisible by 2

Therefore, 224 is divisible by 14

Process to Check If any Number Divisible by 14

Here is the step-by-step procedure to check if a number is divided by 14 or not.

  • Consider any number.
  • Check whether the number is even or odd.
  • If an even number, then double the end digit.
  • Subtract the product from the remaining digits of the number.
  • When the difference is divisible by 7, then the number is divisible by 14.
  • Either the number is odd or the difference is not a multiple of 14 then the number is not divisible by 14
