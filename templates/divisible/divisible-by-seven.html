What is the Divisibility Rule of 7?

In mathematics, the divisibility rule is a method to determine whether the given number is divisible by another foxed number without doing a division calculation. So, here we are providing the divisibility rule of seven.

The 7 divisibility rule states that if a number is divisible by 7, then the difference between the double unit digit of the divisor and the remaining part of the divisor should be a multiple of 7 or equal to 0.

How to Tell if a Number is Divisible by 7?

Here are the tips to tell if a number is divisible by 7 without performing an actual division operation.

  • Get any number which is greater than 7.
  • Take out the unit digit from the given number.
  • Multiply the unit digit of the number by 2.
  • Subtract the remaining portion of the given number from the product obtained in the above step.
  • If the difference is 0 or a multiple of 7, then it is divided by 7.
  • Or, if you get the difference as a large number, then repeat the process until you get a 2-digit number after subtraction.

  1. What are the numbers divisible by 7 from 1 to 100?

  1. What is the divisibility rule of 7?

According to the divisibility rule of 7, the last digit of the given number is multiplied by 2, and the product is subtracted from the rest number. If the obtained value is 0 or a multiple of 7, then the given number is divisible by 7.

  1. Is 273 divisible by 7?

The last digit in the number 273 is 3 and its double is 6. Subtract 27 from 6 i.e 27 - 6 = 21. As 21 is a multiple of 7, 273 is divided by 7.
