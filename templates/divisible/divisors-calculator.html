Definition of Divisor

A divisor is stated as a number that divides another number with or without leaving a remainder. If the integer b is a divisor of the integer a then it is equal to integer c so that c = a/b. Here, c is a non-decimal integer and it represents a division of a by b. However, b is actually a divisor of a.

With equivalence, a is pictured as a multiplication of b and c: a = b x c, such that a is a product of b and c, hence b and c are divisors of a.

What is the definition of Divisors of a Number?

A divisor of an integer number n is a number that splits n with no remainder called a divisor of a number.

Divisor Formula

The formula of divisor can be created for two scenarios called with or without a remainder. So check the divisor formulas for the two conditions below.

Divisor = Dividend ÷ Quotient (With remainder 0)

&

Divisor = (Dividend - Remainder) ÷ Quotient (Without remainder 0)

Solved Example on Divisor Calculations

Example 1:

Find the divisor if the dividend is 59, the quotient is 11 and the remainder is 4.

Solution:

Given inputs

Dividend = 59

Quotient = 11

Remainder = 4

To find the divisor here we have to use the formula of divisor without remainder 0,

Ie., Divisor = (Dividend - Remainder) ÷ Quotient

Divisor = (59 - 4) ÷ 11

Divisor = 55 ÷ 11

Divisor = 5

Hence, the divisor is 5 when the dividend is 59, the quotient is 11 and the remainder is 4.
